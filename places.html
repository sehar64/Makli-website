Makli Thatta is a historical treasure filled with architectural wonders, rich heritage, and cultural significance.

Places to Visit in Makli Thatta

Makli Necropolis

One of the largest cemeteries in the world, housing 500,000+ tombs with stunning architectural beauty.

Shah Jahan Mosque

Built in 1647, this mosque is famous for its unique blue tiles and breathtaking architecture.

Tomb of Isa Khan Hussain

A grand Mughal-era tomb, showcasing intricate carvings and Persian-influenced architecture.

Tomb of Jam Nizamuddin II

A historical monument with intricate stonework and rich Sindhi heritage.

Keenjhar Lake

A picturesque freshwater lake, famous for the legend of Noori Jam Tamachi.

Banbhore

An archaeological site believed to be the ancient city of Debal, linked to the Arab conquest of Sindh.

Mazar Makhdoom Muhammed Hashim Thattvi

He was an Islamic scholar, author, philanthropist, and a spiritual leader who was considered a saint by his followers. He was the first ever translator of the Quran in Sindhi language.

Kalan Kot Fort

Located on Makli Hills south of the Archaeological campus at a distance of about five miles are the extensive remains of a ruined fort once known as “Kalan Kot” or Tughlaqabad.

Haleji Lake

Haleji Lake is a perennial freshwater lake in Thatta District of Sindh Province, Pakistan. It is surrounded by marshes and brackish seepage lagoons.
